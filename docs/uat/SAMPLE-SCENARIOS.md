# Financial RISE Report - Sample Client Scenarios for UAT Testing

**Version:** 1.0
**Date:** 2025-12-22
**For:** Pilot UAT Consultants

---

## Purpose of This Document

These sample client scenarios help you:
1. **Practice** using the Financial RISE tool before conducting real client assessments
2. **Test** different DISC profiles and financial phases
3. **Validate** that reports accurately reflect different client types
4. **Understand** how the system adapts content based on DISC and phase

Each scenario includes a detailed client profile and suggested responses to key assessment questions.

---

## How to Use These Scenarios

### Option 1: Solo Practice Assessment
1. Create a new assessment using the sample client name
2. Answer questions based on the scenario profile provided
3. Generate reports and review results
4. Verify DISC profile and phase determination match expectations

### Option 2: Role-Play with Colleague
1. One person plays "consultant" (you)
2. One person plays "client" using scenario profile
3. Conduct collaborative assessment session
4. Practice communication and question flow
5. Generate and review reports together

### Option 3: Modify for Real Client
1. Use scenario as starting template
2. Adjust details to match your actual client
3. Conduct real assessment with modifications

## Scenario 1: "Struggling Startup Steve"

### Client Profile

**Name:** Steve Johnson
**Company:** TechFlow Solutions LLC
**Industry:** Technology/SaaS
**Years in Business:** 18 months
**Annual Revenue:** $500,000
**Employees:** 3 (including Steve)

**Current Financial Situation:**
- Inconsistent cash flow, frequent cash shortages
- No formal accounting system (uses spreadsheets)
- Personal and business finances are mixed (shared bank account)
- Behind on tax filings (no quarterly estimates paid)
- $75,000 in credit card debt (high interest)
- Co-founder disputes about money management
- No financial reports or metrics tracking

**Business Context:**
- Rapid early growth but struggling with financial chaos
- Focus on product development, neglecting finances
- Steve is technical founder, no financial background
- Revenue is growing but expenses are outpacing it

**Personality (DISC):**
- **Primary:** D (Dominance) - Results-driven, impatient, wants quick solutions
- **Characteristics:** Direct communicator, dislikes details, focused on growth and ROI
- **Communication Style:** "Cut to the chase," wants action items, not theory

### Expected Assessment Results

**Primary Phase:** **Stabilize** (60-70% score)
- Critical needs: Basic accounting, cash flow management, tax compliance
- Stabilization check should flag this client

**Secondary Phase:** Organize (30-40% score)
- Client is thinking about systems but needs stability first

**DISC Profile:** **D (Dominance)** with possible I (Influence) secondary
- High D scores: Quick decision-making, results focus, competitive
- Moderate I scores: Enthusiastic about growth, optimistic

### Sample Question Responses

**Financial Readiness Questions:**

1. **"How often do you review your profit & loss statement?"**
   - **Answer:** Never (no P&L exists)

2. **"Do you have a dedicated business bank account separate from personal finances?"**
   - **Answer:** Shared account

3. **"How confident are you in your current cash flow management?"**
   - **Answer:** 1 (Not confident at all)

4. **"Do you prepare and file quarterly estimated tax payments?"**
   - **Answer:** No

5. **"How much outstanding debt does your business have?"**
   - **Answer:** $50,000+ (credit card debt)

6. **"Do you have a Chart of Accounts set up?"**
   - **Answer:** No / Not sure what that is

7. **"How often do you reconcile your bank accounts?"**
   - **Answer:** Rarely or Never

8. **"Do you have documented financial processes or SOPs?"**
   - **Answer:** No

9. **"Do you create cash flow forecasts?"**
   - **Answer:** No

10. **"How well does your leadership team understand financial reports?"**
    - **Answer:** Not Applicable (no formal reports)

**DISC Questions:**

1. **"When making business decisions, I tend to:"**
   - **Answer:** Make quick decisions based on gut instinct (D trait)

2. **"In client meetings, I prefer to:"**
   - **Answer:** Get straight to the point and focus on results (D trait)

3. **"When facing challenges, I:"**
   - **Answer:** Take charge and find solutions quickly (D trait)

4. **"I am most energized by:"**
   - **Answer:** Achieving goals and seeing measurable results (D trait)

5. **"My communication style is best described as:"**
   - **Answer:** Direct and to the point (D trait)

6. **"When working on projects, I prefer to:"**
   - **Answer:** Move fast and iterate (D/I traits)

7. **"I make decisions primarily based on:"**
   - **Answer:** Logic and results (D/C traits)

8. **"In group settings, I tend to:"**
   - **Answer:** Take the lead and drive action (D trait)

### Expected Report Content

**Consultant Report:**
- DISC: Primary D, possible secondary I
- Phase: Stabilize (with critical flag)
- Action Plan: Immediate focus on separating finances, basic bookkeeping, tax compliance
- Communication Strategy: "Steve needs brief, results-oriented recommendations. Focus on ROI and quick wins. Avoid overwhelming with details."

**Client Report (D-adapted):**
- Brief, bulleted format
- Quick wins: "Separate bank accounts this week. Set up QuickBooks. Schedule tax consultation."
- ROI emphasis: "Fixing these fundamentals will save you $X in taxes and improve cash flow by Y%"
- Direct tone: "Here's what you need to do now..."

### UAT Testing Focus
- Verify critical stabilization flag appears
- Confirm D-type report is concise and results-focused
- Check that action plan prioritizes foundational work first
- Validate quick wins are achievable and high-impact

## Scenario 2: "Organized Olivia"

### Client Profile

**Name:** Olivia Martinez
**Company:** Martinez Advisory Group
**Industry:** Professional Services (Business Consulting)
**Years in Business:** 3 years
**Annual Revenue:** $1.5 million
**Employees:** 6 (Olivia + 5 consultants)

**Current Financial Situation:**
- Solid accounting system (QuickBooks Online)
- Dedicated business bank account (fully separated from personal)
- Quarterly tax payments on track
- Clean books, monthly reconciliations
- Profitability is good, cash flow is stable
- Uses multiple tools that don't integrate (invoicing, time tracking, project management)
- Wants better financial reporting and dashboards
- Ready to implement integrated systems

**Business Context:**
- Graduated from startup phase, now scaling
- Operations are solid but systems are fragmented
- Olivia wants better visibility and automation
- Team is growing, needs systematic processes

**Personality (DISC):**
- **Primary:** C (Compliance) - Analytical, detail-oriented, values accuracy
- **Characteristics:** Systematic thinker, asks lots of questions, wants data
- **Communication Style:** Prefers detailed explanations, likes documentation, thorough

### Expected Assessment Results

**Primary Phase:** **Organize** (65-75% score)
- Needs: System integration, advanced reporting, process documentation

**Secondary Phase:** Build (35-45% score)
- Beginning to think about operational workflows and team SOPs

**DISC Profile:** **C (Compliance)** with possible S (Steadiness) secondary
- High C scores: Analytical thinking, attention to detail, quality focus
- Moderate S scores: Team-oriented, values stability, systematic approach

### Sample Question Responses

**Financial Readiness Questions:**

1. **"How often do you review your profit & loss statement?"**
   - **Answer:** Monthly

2. **"Do you have a dedicated business bank account separate from personal finances?"**
   - **Answer:** Yes, fully separated

3. **"How confident are you in your current cash flow management?"**
   - **Answer:** 4 (Very confident)

4. **"Do you prepare and file quarterly estimated tax payments?"**
   - **Answer:** Yes, on schedule

5. **"How much outstanding debt does your business have?"**
   - **Answer:** Minimal or none

6. **"Do you have a Chart of Accounts set up?"**
   - **Answer:** Yes, well-organized

7. **"How often do you reconcile your bank accounts?"**
   - **Answer:** Monthly

8. **"Are your financial tools integrated (accounting, invoicing, time tracking, etc.)?"**
   - **Answer:** No, using multiple disconnected tools

9. **"Do you have financial dashboards or KPI tracking?"**
   - **Answer:** Basic tracking, want more sophisticated dashboards

10. **"Do you have documented financial processes or SOPs?"**
    - **Answer:** Some documentation, needs formalization

**DISC Questions:**

1. **"When making business decisions, I tend to:"**
   - **Answer:** Analyze data thoroughly before deciding (C trait)

2. **"In client meetings, I prefer to:"**
   - **Answer:** Present detailed information and answer all questions thoroughly (C trait)

3. **"When facing challenges, I:"**
   - **Answer:** Research and analyze before taking action (C trait)

4. **"I am most energized by:"**
   - **Answer:** Solving complex problems with precision (C trait)

5. **"My communication style is best described as:"**
   - **Answer:** Precise and thorough (C trait)

6. **"When working on projects, I prefer to:"**
   - **Answer:** Plan carefully and execute systematically (C/S traits)

7. **"I make decisions primarily based on:"**
   - **Answer:** Data and analysis (C trait)

8. **"In group settings, I tend to:"**
   - **Answer:** Ask clarifying questions and ensure accuracy (C trait)

### Expected Report Content

**Consultant Report:**
- DISC: Primary C, possible secondary S
- Phase: Organize (clear focus on system integration)
- Action Plan: Integrate tools (accounting + invoicing + time tracking), build financial dashboards, document processes
- Communication Strategy: "Olivia needs detailed explanations with data. Provide thorough documentation and step-by-step implementation plans."

**Client Report (C-adapted):**
- Detailed, comprehensive format with data
- Structured roadmap with clear steps
- Data emphasis: "Analysis of your current tools shows X inefficiencies. Integration will save Y hours/month."
- Thorough explanations: "Here's exactly how to implement each step..."
- Includes charts, tables, and metrics

### UAT Testing Focus
- Verify C-type report is detailed and data-rich
- Confirm phase is Organize (not Stabilize or Build)
- Check that action plan focuses on system integration
- Validate report includes sufficient detail for C-type personality

## Scenario 3: "Building Bob"

### Client Profile

**Name:** Bob Chen
**Company:** Chen Manufacturing Inc.
**Industry:** Light Manufacturing
**Years in Business:** 5 years
**Annual Revenue:** $3 million
**Employees:** 15

**Current Financial Situation:**
- Robust accounting system (NetSuite)
- All financial tools integrated
- Strong financial reporting and dashboards
- Team of 3 in finance department
- Operations are smooth but processes are ad-hoc
- Needs documented workflows and SOPs
- Wants to automate manual processes
- Team lacks clear financial roles and responsibilities

**Business Context:**
- Established business with solid foundation
- Systems are in place and integrated
- Ready to build operational excellence
- Wants scalable processes for future growth
- Focus on efficiency and team empowerment

**Personality (DISC):**
- **Primary:** S (Steadiness) - Team-oriented, values stability, supportive
- **Characteristics:** Patient, reliable, collaborative, dislikes rapid change
- **Communication Style:** Prefers step-by-step guidance, values reassurance

### Expected Assessment Results

**Primary Phase:** **Build** (70-80% score)
- Needs: Financial SOPs, team workflows, automation, role clarity

**Secondary Phase:** Grow (30-40% score)
- Beginning to think about strategic planning

**DISC Profile:** **S (Steadiness)** with possible C (Compliance) secondary
- High S scores: Team focus, stability, patient approach
- Moderate C scores: Values structure and documentation

### Sample Question Responses

**Financial Readiness Questions:**

1. **"Are your financial tools integrated?"**
   - **Answer:** Yes, fully integrated (NetSuite ecosystem)

2. **"Do you have financial dashboards or KPI tracking?"**
   - **Answer:** Yes, comprehensive dashboards

3. **"Do you have documented financial processes or SOPs?"**
   - **Answer:** Some documentation, needs expansion and formalization

4. **"Are financial roles and responsibilities clearly defined?"**
   - **Answer:** Somewhat defined, needs clarity

5. **"Do you use automation for financial workflows (approvals, invoicing, etc.)?"**
   - **Answer:** Some automation, many manual processes remain

6. **"Does your team have clear financial workflows?"**
   - **Answer:** Informal workflows, not documented

7. **"Do you create cash flow forecasts?"**
   - **Answer:** Basic forecasting, want more sophisticated planning

8. **"How well does your leadership team understand financial reports?"**
   - **Answer:** 3 (Moderately well)

**DISC Questions:**

1. **"When making business decisions, I tend to:"**
   - **Answer:** Seek input from others and build consensus (S trait)

2. **"In client meetings, I prefer to:"**
   - **Answer:** Listen carefully and provide steady, reliable guidance (S trait)

3. **"When facing challenges, I:"**
   - **Answer:** Take a steady, measured approach (S trait)

4. **"I am most energized by:"**
   - **Answer:** Building strong team relationships (S trait)

5. **"My communication style is best described as:"**
   - **Answer:** Calm and supportive (S trait)

6. **"When working on projects, I prefer to:"**
   - **Answer:** Work collaboratively with clear plans (S/C traits)

7. **"I make decisions primarily based on:"**
   - **Answer:** Team input and proven methods (S trait)

### Expected Report Content

**Consultant Report:**
- DISC: Primary S, possible secondary C
- Phase: Build (focus on operational systems)
- Action Plan: Document financial SOPs, create team workflows, automate processes, clarify roles
- Communication Strategy: "Bob needs step-by-step implementation with team involvement. Reassure about change management. Emphasize stability."

**Client Report (S-adapted):**
- Gentle, reassuring tone
- Step-by-step roadmap with clear stages
- Team emphasis: "We'll work together to build these systems..."
- Change management: "We'll take this one step at a time to ensure smooth transition"
- Supportive language: "You've built a strong foundation. Now let's strengthen it further."

### UAT Testing Focus
- Verify S-type report is reassuring and step-by-step
- Confirm phase is Build (not Organize or Grow)
- Check that action plan focuses on SOPs and workflows
- Validate tone is supportive and non-threatening

## Scenario 4: "Growing Grace"

### Client Profile

**Name:** Grace Thompson
**Company:** Thompson Retail Group
**Industry:** Retail (3 locations)
**Years in Business:** 7 years
**Annual Revenue:** $5 million
**Employees:** 25

**Current Financial Situation:**
- Excellent accounting and operational systems
- Strong team with clear roles
- Documented SOPs for all financial processes
- Automation in place for workflows
- Ready for expansion (2 more locations planned)
- Needs cash flow forecasting for growth
- Wants revenue projections and scenario planning
- Preparing for line of credit application

**Business Context:**
- Established, profitable business
- Operations are running smoothly
- Growth mindset - planning expansion
- Needs strategic financial planning
- Focus on sustainability and smart growth

**Personality (DISC):**
- **Primary:** I (Influence) - Enthusiastic, big-picture thinker, people-focused
- **Characteristics:** Optimistic, collaborative, visionary, loves opportunities
- **Communication Style:** Engaging, storytelling, relationship-driven

### Expected Assessment Results

**Primary Phase:** **Grow** (70-80% score)
- Needs: Cash flow forecasting, revenue projections, scenario planning, growth financing

**Secondary Phase:** Build (40-50% score) or Systemic (20-30%)
- Operations are solid (Build complete)
- Beginning to think about leadership empowerment (Systemic)

**DISC Profile:** **I (Influence)** with possible D (Dominance) secondary
- High I scores: Enthusiasm, collaboration, big-picture thinking
- Moderate D scores: Growth-oriented, results-focused

### Sample Question Responses

**Financial Readiness Questions:**

1. **"Do you have documented financial processes or SOPs?"**
   - **Answer:** Yes, comprehensive documentation

2. **"Are financial workflows automated?"**
   - **Answer:** Yes, majority automated

3. **"Do you create cash flow forecasts?"**
   - **Answer:** Basic 30-day forecasts, want 90-180 day projections

4. **"Do you create revenue projections and business plans?"**
   - **Answer:** Annual budgets, want sophisticated modeling

5. **"Do you conduct scenario planning (best case, worst case, most likely)?"**
   - **Answer:** Not currently, want to implement

6. **"How prepared are you for growth financing (line of credit, loans)?"**
   - **Answer:** Preparing, need better financial projections

7. **"How well does your leadership team understand financial reports?"**
   - **Answer:** 4 (Very well)

8. **"Do you have financial KPIs and dashboards?"**
   - **Answer:** Yes, comprehensive

**DISC Questions:**

1. **"When making business decisions, I tend to:"**
   - **Answer:** Consider big-picture opportunities (I trait)

2. **"In client meetings, I prefer to:"**
   - **Answer:** Build relationships and discuss big-picture ideas (I trait)

3. **"When facing challenges, I:"**
   - **Answer:** Stay optimistic and look for creative solutions (I trait)

4. **"I am most energized by:"**
   - **Answer:** New opportunities and collaboration (I trait)

5. **"My communication style is best described as:"**
   - **Answer:** Enthusiastic and engaging (I trait)

6. **"When working on projects, I prefer to:"**
   - **Answer:** Brainstorm ideas and work collaboratively (I trait)

7. **"I make decisions primarily based on:"**
   - **Answer:** Intuition and opportunities (I trait)

### Expected Report Content

**Consultant Report:**
- DISC: Primary I, possible secondary D
- Phase: Grow (strategic planning focus)
- Action Plan: Implement cash flow forecasting, create revenue projections, build scenario planning models, prepare growth financing package
- Communication Strategy: "Grace responds to big-picture opportunities and collaborative approach. Emphasize growth potential and success stories."

**Client Report (I-adapted):**
- Enthusiastic, engaging tone
- Big-picture vision: "Imagine your business with 5 locations..."
- Collaborative language: "Together, we'll create a roadmap for sustainable growth"
- Success stories and social proof: "Businesses like yours have achieved..."
- Colorful visuals and engaging layout
- Opportunity focus: "Your growth potential is exciting!"

### UAT Testing Focus
- Verify I-type report is enthusiastic and engaging
- Confirm phase is Grow (not Build or Systemic)
- Check that action plan focuses on forecasting and growth planning
- Validate tone is optimistic and opportunity-focused

## Scenario 5: "Systemic Sam"

### Client Profile

**Name:** Sam Patel
**Company:** Patel Professional Services
**Industry:** Professional Services (Law Firm)
**Years in Business:** 10 years
**Annual Revenue:** $10 million
**Employees:** 40 (20 lawyers, 20 support staff)

**Current Financial Situation:**
- World-class financial systems and processes
- CFO on staff, full finance team
- Sophisticated forecasting and planning
- Strong operational excellence
- Partners want to understand financials better
- Leadership team wants financial literacy training
- Need better financial reporting for non-finance leaders
- Want to empower department heads with P&L ownership

**Business Context:**
- Mature, successful business
- All foundational work complete
- Focus on leadership development and empowerment
- Preparing for succession planning
- Want to distribute financial knowledge across leadership

**Personality (DISC):**
- **Primary:** Mixed I/D - Collaborative yet results-driven
- **Characteristics:** Visionary leader, strategic thinker, values both people and results
- **Communication Style:** Big-picture strategy with ROI focus

### Expected Assessment Results

**Primary Phase:** **Systemic** (70-80% score)
- Needs: Financial literacy training, report interpretation, leadership empowerment, KPI ownership

**Secondary Phase:** Grow (40-50% score)
- Strategic planning is ongoing

**DISC Profile:** **I (Influence)** and **D (Dominance)** mixed (no clear primary)
- High I scores: Collaboration, vision, people focus
- High D scores: Results orientation, decisiveness

### Sample Question Responses

**Financial Readiness Questions:**

1. **"Do you create revenue projections and business plans?"**
   - **Answer:** Yes, sophisticated multi-year models

2. **"Do you conduct scenario planning?"**
   - **Answer:** Yes, regularly updated

3. **"How well does your leadership team understand financial reports?"**
   - **Answer:** 3 (Moderately well) - want to improve

4. **"Can your leadership team interpret P&L, balance sheet, and cash flow statements?"**
   - **Answer:** Some can, many need training

5. **"Do department heads have P&L ownership?"**
   - **Answer:** Partially, want to expand

6. **"Do you provide financial literacy training to leadership?"**
   - **Answer:** No, want to implement

7. **"Do non-finance leaders have access to real-time financial dashboards?"**
   - **Answer:** Limited access, want to expand

8. **"Is your organization financially self-sufficient (minimal consultant dependency)?"**
   - **Answer:** CFO handles most, want broader capability

**DISC Questions:**

1. **"When making business decisions, I tend to:"**
   - **Answer:** Balance quick action with team input (I/D mix)

2. **"I am most energized by:"**
   - **Answer:** Mix of achieving goals AND building relationships

3. **"My communication style is best described as:"**
   - **Answer:** Mix of direct and engaging

### Expected Report Content

**Consultant Report:**
- DISC: Mixed I/D (or primary I with strong D secondary)
- Phase: Systemic (financial literacy focus)
- Action Plan: Financial statement training for leadership, create simplified KPI dashboards for department heads, P&L ownership program, financial literacy workshops
- Communication Strategy: "Sam responds to both big-picture vision AND results. Combine collaborative approach with ROI metrics."

**Client Report (I/D mixed adaptation):**
- Balanced tone: Vision + Results
- Collaborative language with ROI emphasis
- Leadership development focus: "Empower your team with financial knowledge"
- Strategic framing: "Building a financially literate leadership team unlocks..."
- Data + Story: Mix of metrics and narrative

### UAT Testing Focus
- Verify Systemic phase is correctly identified
- Confirm mixed DISC profile is handled appropriately
- Check that action plan focuses on financial literacy and empowerment
- Validate report adapts to mixed DISC (not just one type)
