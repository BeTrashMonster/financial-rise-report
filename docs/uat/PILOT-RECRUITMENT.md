## Recruitment Overview

This document outlines the recruitment strategy and criteria for selecting 5-10 pilot consultants to participate in User Acceptance Testing (UAT) for the Financial RISE Report application.

---

## Selection Criteria

### Required Qualifications
1. **Professional Background:**
   - Fractional CFO, accountant, bookkeeper, or financial advisor
   - Minimum 2 years of client-facing experience
   - Active client base (at least 5 current clients)

2. **Technical Readiness:**
   - Comfortable using web-based applications
   - Reliable internet connection and modern browser (Chrome, Firefox, Safari, Edge)
   - Laptop or desktop computer for assessment sessions
   - Willingness to test on multiple devices (desktop, laptop, tablet)

3. **Availability:**
   - Available for 2-week UAT period (dates TBD)
   - Can commit to conducting 2-3 complete assessments during UAT
   - Available for feedback sessions (1-2 hours/week)
   - Able to respond to surveys and provide written feedback

4. **Communication:**
   - Strong written communication skills for providing detailed feedback
   - Comfortable with video calls for feedback sessions
   - Responsive to email and Slack communication

### Preferred Qualifications
- Experience with financial readiness assessments or business consulting frameworks
- Familiarity with DISC personality profiling
- Experience providing feedback on software products
- Diverse client base (various industries, business sizes)
- Interest in early adoption of new tools

### Diversity Goals
To ensure comprehensive testing, recruit pilot consultants with:
- **Geographic diversity:** Different US states/time zones
- **Client diversity:** Small businesses (< $1M revenue), mid-size ($1M-$10M), larger ($10M+)
- **Industry diversity:** Professional services, retail, manufacturing, healthcare, technology
- **Experience diversity:** Mix of veteran (10+ years) and newer (2-5 years) consultants
- **Practice diversity:** Solo practitioners, small firms, part of larger organizations

## Recruitment Strategy

### Phase 1: Outreach Planning (Week 1)
1. **Develop Recruitment Materials:**
   - Email outreach template
   - Pilot program overview document
   - Consent and NDA forms
   - Pilot benefits and expectations

2. **Identify Recruitment Channels:**
   - Professional networks (LinkedIn, industry associations)
   - Fractional CFO communities and forums
   - Accounting/bookkeeping associations (AICPA, NACPB)
   - Personal network referrals
   - Industry conference contacts

3. **Create Screening Process:**
   - Application form (Google Forms or Typeform)
   - Screening call script (15-minute phone/video call)
   - Selection rubric

### Phase 2: Active Recruitment (Week 2-3)
1. **Send Initial Outreach:**
   - Target 20-30 potential pilots
   - Personalized email with pilot program overview
   - Application link

2. **Conduct Screening:**
   - Review applications (target: 15-20 qualified applicants)
   - Schedule screening calls
   - Assess fit based on criteria

3. **Select Final Cohort:**
   - Select 8-12 pilots (buffer for potential dropouts)
   - Send acceptance notifications
   - Collect signed NDAs and consent forms

### Phase 3: Onboarding (Week 4)
1. **Kick-off Meeting:**
   - Virtual group orientation (1 hour)
   - Product demo and walkthrough
   - UAT timeline and expectations
   - Q&A session

2. **Individual Setup:**
   - Provide test account credentials
   - Send user guide and reference materials
   - Add to Slack feedback channel
   - Schedule individual check-in calls

## Pilot Consultant Responsibilities

**Expected Commitments:**
1. **Testing Activities:**
   - Conduct 2-3 complete assessments with actual clients (or role-play scenarios)
   - Test all features: assessment creation, questionnaire, report generation, PDF download
   - Test on multiple devices/browsers if possible
   - Report bugs and issues via feedback channels

2. **Feedback Provision:**
   - Complete weekly feedback surveys (10-15 minutes)
   - Participate in 1-2 feedback interviews (30-60 minutes each)
   - Provide written feedback in Slack channel
   - Share client reactions and testimonials (with permission)

3. **Communication:**
   - Respond to emails and Slack messages within 24-48 hours
   - Attend scheduled check-in calls
   - Report critical issues immediately

4. **Confidentiality:**
   - Sign NDA covering product features and client data
   - Do not share credentials or access with unauthorized users
   - Follow data privacy guidelines for client information

### Screening Call Script

**Duration:** 15 minutes

**Introduction (2 minutes)**
- Thank applicant for their interest
- Briefly introduce Financial RISE Report
- Confirm they've reviewed the application details

**Qualification Questions (8 minutes)**
1. "Tell me about your current consulting practice. How many clients do you typically work with?"
2. "Walk me through how you currently assess a new client's financial health. What tools or frameworks do you use?"
3. "How comfortable are you with adopting new software tools? Can you give an example of a recent tool you've started using?"
4. "For this pilot, we'll need you to conduct 2-3 full assessments during a 2-week period. Does that feel feasible with your current schedule?"
5. "We'll be asking for detailed feedback through surveys, Slack, and interviews. Are you comfortable providing constructive criticism and suggestions?"
6. "Have you worked with personality assessments like DISC before? If not, are you interested in learning?"

**Pilot Program Details (3 minutes)**
- Explain UAT timeline and expectations
- Describe feedback channels (Slack, surveys, video interviews)
- Mention benefits (6-month free access, training, networking)
- Clarify NDA and confidentiality requirements

**Q&A and Next Steps (2 minutes)**
- Answer any questions from applicant
- Explain selection timeline: "We'll be making final selections by [date] and will notify all applicants"
- Thank them for their time

**Post-Call Notes:**
- Rate on selection criteria (1-5 scale):
  - Professional fit (relevant experience, active practice)
  - Technical readiness (comfort with software, devices available)
  - Availability (realistic commitment, schedule alignment)
  - Communication (articulate, engaged, constructive)
  - Diversity fit (adds geographic, industry, or experience diversity)
- Overall recommendation: Yes / Maybe / No
- Notes for selection committee

## Risk Mitigation

**Potential Risks:**
1. **Insufficient Applications:**
   - Mitigation: Cast wider net, extend outreach period, offer stronger incentives

2. **Low Commitment/Dropout:**
   - Mitigation: Over-recruit (12 vs. 10 target), set clear expectations upfront, regular check-ins

3. **Lack of Diversity:**
   - Mitigation: Targeted outreach to underrepresented segments, referral requests for specific profiles

4. **Technical Issues:**
   - Mitigation: Pre-UAT technical check, IT support on standby, browser compatibility testing

5. **Poor Feedback Quality:**
   - Mitigation: Structured feedback templates, guided interviews, specific prompts, incentivize detailed responses
